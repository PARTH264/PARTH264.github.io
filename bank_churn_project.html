Query 6 — High Credit Limit but Low Balance (Balance < 20% of Limit)
bank_churn_analysis.sql Q6 
SELECT TOP 10
  clientnum, customer_age,
  income, credit_limit,
  balance,
  ROUND((balance * 100.0 / credit_limit), 2)
      AS Balance_Percentage
FROM bank_churn_data
WHERE balance < (0.2 * credit_limit)

Queries 8–10

Risk Flagging & VIP Segmentation

Query 9 — Credit Utilization Risk Classification
bank_churn_analysis.sql Q9 
SELECT
  clientnum, customer_age,
  income, credit_limit,
  utilization_ratio,
  ROUND((utilization_ratio * 100.0), 2)
      AS Utilization_Percentage,
  CASE
    WHEN utilization_ratio > 0.8
        THEN 'High Risk'
    WHEN utilization_ratio
        BETWEEN 0.5 AND 0.8
        THEN 'Moderate Risk'
    ELSE 'Low Risk'
  END AS Risk_Level
FROM bank_churn_data
ORDER BY utilization_ratio DESC;

Findings

Key Insights from SQL Analysis

💳
Blue Cards Dominate at 93.2%
Of 10,127 customers, 9,436 (93.2%) hold Blue cards. Only 555 Silver, 116 Gold and 20 Platinum. This extreme concentration in the lowest tier signals an opportunity to upsell mid-tier Silver/Gold cards to eligible customers.
⚠️
Near-100% Utilization = High Churn Risk
Top utilization customers reach 0.999 utilization ratio (99.9%) with credit limits as low as $1,438. These customers are maxed out — a classic churn precursor. Proactive credit limit review or hardship outreach is critical.
🏦
$0 Balance on High-Limit Cards = Silent Churners
Query 6 revealed customers with credit limits above $29,000–$34,000 holding $0 balance. These are disengaged, high-value customers actively not using the card — the most dangerous churn segment to retain.
👨‍👩‍👧
Lower Income = More Dependents
Customers earning less than $40K have an average of 2.42 dependents vs just 2.08 for $120K+ earners. Higher dependent burden correlates with lower income — informing responsible credit limit assignment and risk appetite.
👑
VIP Segment: 4+ Year Tenure Customers
Customers with months_on_book > 53 (4.4+ years) represent the most loyal segment. These VIPs carry healthy balances ($1,800–$2,500) and high credit limits. Dedicated retention programs for this cohort would maximise lifetime value.
📊
46-55 Age Group is the Core Customer Base
The 46-55 age band is the largest segment with 3,762 customers (37.1%), followed by 36-45 at 3,086. The 18-25 segment has only 283 customers — an underserved demographic with growth potential for younger credit products.
