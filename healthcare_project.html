Analysis

Key Insights & Findings

⚠️
37% Coverage Gap Identified
Out of 6,597 eligible members, 2,413 (37%) did not receive screening. This gap represents a major opportunity for targeted outreach campaigns to improve preventive care coverage.
🌍
USCA Dominates at 77.4%
The USCA region accounts for 77.4% (5.1K) of all screening-eligible members. APAC (12.08%) and EMEA (9.96%) are secondary markets that may require region-specific strategies.
🗣️
English Dominates at 73%
English-speaking members represent 4,837 of eligibles. However, 10+ non-English languages (Russian, Korean, Spanish) highlight the need for multilingual outreach to close screening gaps.
🧬
White Race = Largest Group
White members (3,207) form the largest racial group at 48.6%, followed by Unknown (23.3%) and Asian (13.4%). The 23% "Unknown" category signals data quality improvement opportunities.
📊
Ethnicity Data Quality Issue
23.45% of members have "Unknown" ethnicity — this data quality gap reduces the effectiveness of targeted outreach. A data collection improvement initiative is recommended.
🎯
Actionable Recommendation
Prioritize the 2,413 unscreened members by region (APAC + EMEA first) and language group. Multilingual reminder campaigns could significantly improve the 63% screening rate.
