Analysis

Key Insights & Findings

🌐
Online TA Dominates Segments
Online Travel Agencies generate $137.1L — by far the largest segment. This heavy reliance on OTAs (like Booking.com) inflates commission costs and signals an opportunity to grow Direct bookings.
🏙️
City Hotel Leads Resort Hotel
City Hotel accounts for 55.37% ($14.4M) vs Resort Hotel at 44.63% ($11.6M). City Hotel's business traveler volume drives higher weekday occupancy, contributing to the $18.9M weeknight revenue split.
🌙
Weeknights Outperform Weekends
Weeknight revenue ($18.9M) is 2.66× higher than weekend revenue ($7.1M). This suggests primarily corporate/business guests. A weekend leisure promotion strategy could significantly boost RevPAR.
🚫
44K Cancellations = Revenue Leak
With 44,000 cancelled bookings out of 255K total days booked, the cancellation rate is ~17.3%. Implementing stricter cancellation policies or non-refundable rate plans could recover significant lost revenue.
👤
Transient Guests = 74% of Revenue
Transient customers alone generate $19.3M (74% of total revenue). Investing in loyalty programs to convert transient guests to repeat direct-booking customers would reduce OTA dependency considerably.
🛏️
Room A is the Revenue Engine
Room type A dominates at $14.5M (55.8%) of all room-type revenue. Upselling Room D ($6.0M) and E ($2.5M) through targeted pricing strategies could diversify revenue and improve ADR.
