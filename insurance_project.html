Analysis

Key Insights & Risk Findings

🚗
Private Cars Drive 80% of Claims
Private vehicle use accounts for $150.4M (80.08%) of total claims vs Commercial at $37.41M (19.92%). This signals that personal auto policies carry the bulk of risk exposure and should be prioritized in pricing models.
🏆
Ford & Chevrolet = Highest Risk Makes
Ford leads at $17M and Chevrolet at $15M — together accounting for ~$32M or 17% of total claims. These high-volume domestic makes likely correlate with higher accident rates or older vehicle age in the portfolio.
👶
Kids Driving Dramatically Reduces Claims
Households with 0 kids driving claim $134M vs just $4M for 3 kids. This strongly suggests single/young drivers without family obligations are significantly higher risk — a critical factor for premium segmentation.
🎓
Bachelors Education = Highest Risk Segment
Bachelor's degree holders account for $94.0M — 50% of all claims. Interestingly, higher education (PhD = $13.7M, 7.29%) correlates with significantly lower claims, suggesting a meaningful risk-education relationship to leverage in underwriting.
💍
Single Policyholders = Highest Claim Group
Single individuals filed $96.3M (51.3%) of all claims — nearly double married policyholders ($50.6M). This demographic should receive adjusted risk premiums. The single + High School segment alone accounts for $40.2M.
🗺️
Perfect Geographic Balance — No Zone Bias
All 5 coverage zones (Highly Urban, Urban, Suburban, Rural, Highly Rural) show nearly identical ~20% claim share (~$37–38M each). This rare balance suggests either geographic diversification strategy or uniform risk pricing across zones.
